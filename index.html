Course overview

ED 401 develops a rigorous foundation in modern analysis. Students study completeness, compactness and connectedness in metric spaces; foundational theorems of functional analysis; duality and geometry in Banach and Hilbert spaces; and the spectrum of compact and bounded operators.

Prerequisite: Third-year mathematics courses. Co-requisite: None.

Course outcomes

CO1 · Metric spaces

Explain metric-space structure, convergence, continuity, Cauchy sequences and completeness.

CO2 · Compactness

Apply compactness, connectedness, Baire category and Arzelà–Ascoli results.

CO3 · Banach spaces

Analyze normed spaces, bounded operators, duality and fundamental Banach-space theorems.

CO4 · Hilbert spaces

Use duality, Riesz representation, adjoints and classes of operators in Hilbert spaces.

CO5 · Spectral theory

Determine spectra and apply spectral theorems to compact, self-adjoint, normal and unitary operators.

Frequently asked questions

What background is expected?

Comfort with real analysis, sequences and series, linear algebra, and proof writing at third-year level.

What happens in tutorials?

Tutorials emphasize theorem-proof strategies, counterexamples, operator calculations and structured problem solving.

How can this site connect to a classroom platform?
